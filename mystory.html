From Chicago to Denver

My culinary journey across 1,000 miles and countless flavors

Windy City Beginnings

My love affair with food started in my grandmother's Chicago kitchen, where the smells of simmering tomato sauce and freshly baked bread were as constant as Lake Michigan's breeze. By 16, I was washing dishes at a local Italian joint, sneaking bites of handmade pasta when the chef wasn't looking.

After culinary school, I worked my way up through Chicago's competitive restaurant scene - from pantry chef at a Michelin-starred spot to running the line at a beloved neighborhood bistro. The energy, the pressure, the late nights fueled by espresso and dreams - it shaped me.

But after 15 years, I needed a change. The mountains were calling.

Rocky Mountain Revelation

Denver surprised me. The food scene wasn't just about green chili and bison burgers (though both are delicious). I discovered innovative chefs blending global techniques with local ingredients, craft breweries pushing boundaries, and a warmth that reminded me of Chicago's hospitality.

As I explored, I started sharing my finds with friends back home - photos of perfect pork belly bao, descriptions of mole that made me reconsider Mexican cuisine, late-night texts about a burger that might rival Au Cheval's. That's how The Real Bear was born.

This blog is my love letter to Denver's food scene - from the fine dining to the food trucks, with Chicago roots that still influence how I taste and critique.

What You'll Find Here

Expect honest reviews from a chef's perspective - I'll tell you when the risotto is perfectly al dente or if the duck could use more resting time. But always with respect for the craft.

You'll get recipes that blend my Chicago training with Colorado ingredients - think deep dish with hatch chiles or elevated brats with local craft beer mustard.

Mostly, I hope to share the joy I find in Denver's growing food scene - the chefs, the servers, the farmers, and the flavors that make this city special.
